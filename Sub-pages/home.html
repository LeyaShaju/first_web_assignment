Kerala

Kerala, also known as God's own country, is located on the southern coastline of India. It is renowned for its greenery, serene backwaters, spectacular beaches, and rich cultural heritage. Kerala is also well known for its Ayurvedic treatment and festivals like Onam. In terms of arts, Kerala has created 2 of the 7 Indian classical dances, Mohiniyattam and Kathakali. It is one of the most secular and pluralistic states in India with the highest literacy rate. Don't forget the amazing cuisine featuring coconuts, various spices, and great seafood.

Kerala has the highest literacy rate of 96.2% in India, and homes the first 100% literate town in Kottayam. English has been made compulsory in schools in Kerala and so around 80& of the state has basic profiency in reading, writing and speaking in English. Overall if you visit kerala, you can easily communicate in English especially in urban and tourist locations. Kerala is divided into 14 Districts. Alapuzha, Ernakulam, Idukki, Kannur,Kasargod,Kochi(Formerly kown as Cochin), Kozhikode, Kottayam, Malappuram, Pathanamthitta, Palakkad, thrissur, Thiruvananthapuram (The capital) and Wayanad.

If you do plan on going to Kerala when its the warmest. Make sure to go between March and May, where you can also celebrate "Vishu" (a hindu festival filled with Joy) and Thrissur pooram, located in thrissur. If you prefer not to face the raging sun try going between december and February when it's the coolest. There will be a lot of kathakali performances and snake boat races going on in that time. between June and September would be monsoon season. Heavy rain, however within the greenary of kerala. The rain looks maginifcant. In that time, Kerala hosts their biggest festivals "onam" also known as the harvest festival. Celebrated by all religions and communities. Onam lasts for 10 days and they festival is filled with rituals, dances, boat races and their famous sadhya.
